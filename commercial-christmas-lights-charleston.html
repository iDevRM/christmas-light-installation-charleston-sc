What Does Commercial Installation Involve?

We design a lighting layout scaled to your building — storefront windows, entryways, rooflines, trees, or an entire shopping plaza — using commercial-grade LED strands rated for longer runs and heavier outdoor use than typical residential lights.

Because commercial properties often need to stay open and presentable during installation, we schedule around your business hours and work with property managers or facilities staff directly on access, timing, and any building requirements.

When Do You Need Commercial Installation?

  • Your storefront or office looks dark next to lit-up neighbors during the holiday shopping season.
  • You manage multiple properties and want one vendor handling installation and takedown across all of them.
  • Your building has height or access challenges that make in-house staff installation impractical or unsafe.
  • You want a display that survives the whole season without staff climbing ladders to fix dead strands.

Why Does Commercial Lighting Need a Different Approach?

A single-home roofline is a straightforward job. A storefront facade, multi-story office building, or shopping plaza involves more square footage, more access points, and often stricter safety and insurance requirements than a residential job. Commercial-grade lights are also built to handle longer continuous circuits without voltage drop dimming the far end of a run.

Business hours add another layer — installation and any mid-season repairs usually need to happen before opening or after closing so customers and foot traffic aren't disrupted.

What Affects the Cost of Commercial Installation in Charleston?

Commercial pricing varies more than residential because properties vary so much in size and complexity. Typical factors:

  • Square footage and building height. A single storefront costs far less to light than a multi-building property.
  • Access equipment needed. Taller buildings may require lift equipment rather than standard ladders.
  • Number of separate structures. Shopping plazas and multi-building offices are quoted per property or as a package.
  • After-hours scheduling. Evening or early-morning installation to avoid disrupting business hours.

Most commercial jobs run $800 to $5,000 or more depending on scope. Call for a walkthrough and firm quote — we don't price commercial jobs sight unseen.

Single Property vs. Multi-Location: Which Do You Need?

If you manage one building, a single quote and installation visit covers everything. If you manage several properties — a management company with multiple storefronts, or an office park with several buildings — we can bundle scheduling and invoicing across all of them under one account, which is usually simpler and more cost-effective than coordinating separate vendors per site.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you work with property management companies?

Yes. We coordinate directly with property managers or facilities staff for scheduling, access, and invoicing, and can handle multiple properties under one account if you manage more than one building or shopping center.

Can installation happen after business hours?

Yes, for storefronts and offices that need to stay open during the day, we can schedule installation in the evening or early morning so it doesn't interfere with customers or staff.

Are commercial-grade lights different from residential ones?

Commercial-grade LED strands are built for longer continuous runs, higher outdoor durability ratings, and more consistent brightness across a large building facade, which matters more at commercial scale than on a single home's roofline.

Do you carry insurance for commercial properties?

Yes, we're licensed and insured, and can provide a certificate of insurance to your property manager or landlord before work begins if your building requires one.
