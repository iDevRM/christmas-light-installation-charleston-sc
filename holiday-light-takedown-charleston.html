What Does Light Takedown Involve?

We remove every strand, clip, and wreath from your roofline, windows, doorway, and landscaping, checking each section for leftover clips or hardware so nothing is left behind to damage trim or gutters over the year.

Every strand gets coiled and labeled by section before it's boxed, so if you use our storage service, next year's installation starts from an organized, tested set instead of a tangled bin pulled out of the attic.

When Do You Need Takedown Service?

  • The holidays are over and you don't want to climb a ladder in January cold.
  • Someone else installed your lights and you just want a clean, professional removal.
  • You want your lights properly stored instead of stuffed in a bin that turns into next year's tangled mess.
  • Your lights have been up for a while and clips or strands are starting to loosen or sag.

Why Does Timely Takedown Matter?

Lights left up too long fade faster in direct sun and UV exposure, and clips that started snug in November can loosen over months of wind and rain, sometimes catching on gutters or shingles as they shift. Charleston's spring pollen and storm season also make an already-loose display more likely to come down on its own instead of being removed cleanly.

Scheduling takedown in January, rather than letting a display linger into spring, keeps both your lights and your roofline in better shape for next season.

What Affects the Cost of Takedown in Charleston?

Most takedown jobs run $150 to $400, and pricing depends on:

  • How much was installed. A roofline-only display costs less to remove than a full package with trees and shrubs.
  • Whether you want storage included. Coiling, labeling, and storing adds a bit to the base takedown price.
  • Roofline height and access. Steep or multi-story homes take a bit longer to work around safely.

Takedown Only vs. Takedown & Storage: Which Do You Need?

Takedown only makes sense if you'd rather keep your own lights at home, or you're not sure yet whether you'll book installation again next year. We remove and neatly box everything for you to store yourself.

Takedown with storage is the better fit if you want next season to start simple — your lights come back already tested, coiled, and labeled by section, and installation next year moves faster since we already know your layout.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you take down lights someone else installed?

Yes. Takedown is available as a standalone service even if we didn't install your lights originally. We'll remove everything the same way we would our own installation, with no damage to your roof, gutters, or siding.

When should I schedule takedown?

Most homeowners schedule takedown in early-to-mid January, after the holidays but before lights start visibly fading in the sun. We book takedown appointments through late January, but earlier is easier to schedule than mid-month.

What does storage include?

We coil each strand, label it by section (roofline, windows, trees, etc.), and store it in labeled bins until next season. When you book installation again next year, we already know exactly what you have and where it goes.

Can I just get takedown without storage?

Yes, takedown and storage are priced separately. Some homeowners prefer to keep their own lights at home; we're happy to just remove and neatly box everything for you to store yourself.
