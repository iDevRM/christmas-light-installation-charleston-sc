What Is Christmas Light Installation in Charleston?

Christmas light installation in Charleston means a professional crew designs, hangs, maintains, and eventually removes a custom holiday lighting display on your home or business — rooflines, windows, doorways, trees, and shrubs — using commercial-grade LED lights and clips instead of the store-bought strands most homeowners fight with every year.

It's the service homeowners call when they want the polished, magazine-style look of a fully lit roofline and landscape without spending a weekend on an extension ladder, untangling last year's strands, or discovering half the lights are burned out after they're already hung.

When Do You Need Christmas Light Installation?

  • You want your lights up before Thanksgiving without giving up a weekend to do it yourself.
  • Your home has a steep, multi-story, or historic roofline. Charleston's single-house piazzas, steep metal roofs, and older trim aren't safe or simple DIY territory.
  • You're tired of tangled, half-working strands pulled out of a box every December, with no way to know which bulbs still work until they're already up.
  • You're hosting holiday guests, a party, or an open house and want the exterior looking its best without last-minute ladder work.
  • You don't want to store, test, or climb for lights again — we handle setup and takedown so you never touch a bin of lights.

How Christmas Light Installation Works in Charleston

  1. Free quote and design consultation. We look at photos of your home or do a quick walkthrough and design a layout — roofline, windows, trees, shrubs, wreaths — before you commit to anything.
  2. Custom color and coverage plan. Warm white, multicolor, or a specific palette to match your home; you approve the design before installation day.
  3. Professional installation with commercial-grade clips. No nails, staples, or adhesive on your roof, gutters, or siding — everything attaches and removes cleanly.
  4. Season-long maintenance included. If a bulb or strand fails, we come fix it at no extra charge through New Year's Day.
  5. Takedown after the season. We remove everything, coil and label the strands, and can store them until next year if you'd like.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Christmas light installation cost in Charleston?

A roofline-only package on an average single-family home usually runs $350 to $800. A full package that adds windows, doorways, shrubs, and a wreath typically lands between $600 and $1,500. Larger homes, steep or multi-story rooflines, and custom color designs push toward the higher end. We give you an exact number after seeing photos of your home or a quick walkthrough, not a vague estimate.

Do you provide the lights, or do I use my own?

We provide commercial-grade LED lights as part of every installation, which last longer and look more uniform than most store-bought sets. If you already own lights you love, we're happy to install those instead and simply charge for labor, though we can't guarantee older strands against mid-season failure the way we can with our own commercial-grade lights.

What happens if a bulb or strand fails during the season?

Every installation includes free maintenance through New Year's Day. If a bulb burns out or a strand goes dark, call us and we'll come fix it, usually within a day or two, at no extra charge. You should never be stuck staring at a dark section of roofline through the holidays.

Do you take the lights down after the holidays?

Yes, takedown is available as an add-on or a standalone service if someone else installed your lights. We remove everything, coil and label the strands, and can store them for you until next season so you're not the one climbing a ladder in January.

How far in advance should I book installation?

Late October through mid-November is the best window to book if you want your lights up before Thanksgiving. Charleston's installation calendar fills up fast in early December, so waiting until after Thanksgiving often means a later install date than you'd like.

Will installation damage my roof or siding?

No. We use commercial-grade clips that attach to gutters, shingles, and trim without nails, staples, or adhesive, so there's nothing left behind to patch or repair. This is especially important on Charleston's historic homes, where original woodwork and slate or metal roofing shouldn't be punctured for a seasonal display.
