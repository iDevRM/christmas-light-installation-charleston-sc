What Does Residential Installation Involve?

We design a layout for your roofline, windows, doorway, and any trees or shrubs you'd like included, using commercial-grade LED lights and clips that attach without nails or staples. You approve the color scheme and coverage before we ever climb a ladder.

Installation itself typically takes a single visit. We test every strand before we leave, walk the property with you (or send photos if you're not home), and confirm the display looks the way you expected before calling the job done.

When Do You Need Residential Installation?

  • Your roofline is steep, multi-story, or hard to reach safely — common on Charleston's historic single-house and raised-foundation homes.
  • You want your lights up before Thanksgiving without spending a weekend on it yourself.
  • You're tired of tangled strands and dead bulbs discovered only after they're already hung.
  • You want a polished, uniform look instead of mismatched store-bought sets.

Why Does DIY Installation Go Wrong So Often?

Most DIY Christmas light problems come down to two things: unsafe ladder work on rooflines that weren't designed for it, and lights that were never tested before going up. Charleston's historic homes add a third complication — older trim and slate or metal roofing that can be damaged by nails, staples, or the wrong kind of clip.

By the time most homeowners notice a dark strand or a loose clip, it's already mid-December and re-climbing the ladder isn't appealing. A professional crew tests every strand beforehand and uses attachment methods built for the specific roofline, so those problems don't happen in the first place.

What Affects the Cost of Residential Installation in Charleston?

Most residential jobs fall between $350 and $1,500, and where your home lands in that range depends on:

  • Roofline length and stories. A single-story ranch costs less to outline than a two-story home with dormers.
  • How much beyond the roofline you want covered. Windows, doorways, trees, and shrubs each add to the layout.
  • Color scheme and complexity. A single warm-white outline is simpler than a multicolor custom design with accent trees.
  • Access and safety factors. Steep or historic rooflines sometimes need extra time to work around safely.

Roofline-Only vs. Full Package: Which Do You Need?

A roofline-only package outlines the main roof edge and gables — it's the most popular choice for homeowners who want a clean, classic look without a big investment, usually $350 to $800 for an average home.

A full package adds windows, doorway trim, wreaths, and landscape lighting for trees and shrubs, creating the fuller, magazine-style look many homeowners picture when they think of professionally lit homes. If you're hosting guests or simply want the most complete display, the full package is the better fit; if budget or a simpler look matters more, roofline-only still makes a strong statement.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does residential installation take?

Most single-family homes take 1.5 to 3 hours from setup to final walkthrough, depending on roofline length and how much of the yard, windows, and shrubs you want included. Larger or multi-story homes with a full custom design can run longer.

Can you match my home's existing decor or color scheme?

Yes. We design around your home's trim color, brick or siding tone, and any existing wreaths or decorations, and you approve the layout and color scheme before installation day. Warm white is the most common choice for Charleston's historic homes, but multicolor and custom palettes are just as easy.

Do I need to be home during installation?

No, as long as we have access to your exterior and an outdoor outlet. Many homeowners simply confirm the design by phone or email in advance and let us handle the rest while they're at work.

What if I want to add more lights partway through the season?

Just call us. We can usually add an accent tree, extra window trim, or a wreath within a few days, and since we already have your layout on file, it's a quick add-on rather than a full redesign.
